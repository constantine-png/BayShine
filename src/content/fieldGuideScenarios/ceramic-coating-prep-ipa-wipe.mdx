Ceramic coating chemistry does not distinguish between the clear coat you want it to bond to and everything sitting on top of that surface. Polish oils, wax residue, fingerprints, silicone from a spray detailer – the coating bonds to whatever is present. The protection is already compromised before it cures.

The IPA panel wipe eliminates that variable. It is not a formality at the end of the prep sequence. It is the step that determines whether the coating actually adheres.

## What the Panel Wipe Is Removing

After paint correction, the surface looks clean. Under direct light it looks refined. But polishing leaves behind oils from the compound or polish carrier, and those oils are invisible to a visual inspection. They surface later as uneven gloss, early delamination, or reduced water behavior in a coating that should have lasted years.

Beyond polish oils, any vehicle that has been waxed or treated with a spray sealant carries residual silicone and carnauba deposits. Clay bar and iron fallout decontamination remove surface contamination – they do not remove lighter chemical residues. The IPA wipe addresses what decontamination leaves behind.

Fingerprints are also a real factor. Every time a hand crosses a panel during inspection, skin oils are deposited. A single handprint can produce a visible adhesion void in a cured coating, showing up as a dull patch under raking light after the coating locks in.

## Dilution: Why 70 to 90 Percent Matters

The correct IPA dilution for a panel wipe is 70 to 90 percent isopropyl alcohol. The water component matters.

Below 70 percent, the water concentration slows evaporation enough that residual moisture can interact with coating chemistry if application follows quickly. Above 90 percent, evaporation is so rapid that the solution lifts residue and flashes before a complete wipe pass removes it, redistributing contamination rather than eliminating it.

At 70 to 90 percent, the evaporation rate allows a full, deliberate wipe pass to complete before the surface dries. In Tampa Bay during summer, panels in direct sun reach surface temperatures well above ambient air temperature. On a hot panel, even correctly diluted IPA evaporates faster than it would in a controlled environment. Work in shade, work in morning hours, and keep panels out of direct sun before application.

## The Correct Wipe Technique

One direction. One pass. This is not open to interpretation.

Fold [The Roman](/go/the-roman) into a quarter-fold to give yourself a firm, flat working face. The 300 GSM waffle weave construction is right for this step – the texture creates enough mechanical action to lift residue without depositing anything back, and the weave holds what it removes rather than redistributing it across the panel.

Apply IPA to the towel face, not to the panel. Misting directly onto a hot panel in Pasco County or North Hillsborough summer conditions means the solution is evaporating before the towel makes contact.

Wipe in one direction – left to right or top to bottom – in a single overlapping pass. Do not scrub. Do not use circular motions. Do not return to a section with the same towel face. Rotate to a clean face before moving to the next panel. A face that has already wiped one panel carries the oils it removed. Using it again deposits that contamination on a surface you just cleaned.

Work one panel at a time and apply the coating immediately after the IPA flashes off, typically 30 to 60 seconds under Florida humidity. Do not wipe the full vehicle and then start coating. By the time you reach the last panel, the first panels have accumulated airborne fallout and any fingerprints introduced during staging.

## What Happens When This Step Is Skipped

Adhesion failures from contaminated substrates are not always obvious at cure time. A coating applied over polish oils may look correct during application and show normal water behavior in the first weeks. The failure appears later as delamination in isolated sections, or as one panel showing significantly less hydrophobic behavior than adjacent areas.

At that point, the coating must be removed, the surface re-corrected, and the entire process repeated from scratch. The IPA wipe costs five minutes. The remediation costs the full job.

In high-UV environments like the Tampa Bay area, a compromised bond degrades faster than in temperate climates. Florida UV load and heat cycling throughout most of the year accelerate every weakness in a coating's foundation. Getting the prep right is not optional when the environment is this demanding.

## When to Use a Professional Application

If the vehicle went through multi-stage correction or carried prior protection layers requiring full decontamination, the prep sequence is longer than a single IPA wipe covers. Professional applications include full decontamination, correction, and a controlled environment that removes the ambient humidity and temperature variables that complicate DIY work in Florida conditions.
