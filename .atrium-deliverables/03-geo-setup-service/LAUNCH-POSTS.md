## Response templates

### "Why $2,500 and not $5,000 or $10,000?"

```
Fair question. The benchmark: solo consultants offering comparable scope (schema + llms.txt + internal linking + rewrites + tracking) typically charge $1,500-3,500. Agencies start at $5,000-15,000 for the same work. The $2,500 price puts this in the affordable-professional tier, which matches a no-track-record cold-start positioning. Once there's a portfolio of completed engagements, the price moves up.
```

### "Can I see your work on another client first?"

```
Yes. The BayShine Detailing site (bayshine.net) is the dogfood case study. The work ran end-to-end against that site over the past month, and the 12% Perplexity citation improvement and 8% ChatGPT search improvement are documented at {{CASE_STUDY_URL}}. After the first paying engagement, that case study becomes the second reference; case studies compound as the catalog grows.
```

### "What if my CMS is custom?"

```
The deliverables are platform-agnostic: validated JSON-LD (paste into your head), llms.txt (upload to your root), Markdown rewrites of your pages, a CSV of internal-linking additions. Anyone with admin access to your site can implement them. For platforms with limited custom-static-file support (Webflow, Squarespace), the llms.txt portion has documented workarounds.
```

### "Can I get a custom quote?"

```
The service is fixed-scope at $2,500. Custom scope is handled by a different engagement type. If your site has a meaningfully larger scope (a multi-location service business with 25+ pages of service-area content, an existing SEO consultant we'd coordinate with, a vertical we haven't built calibration for), reply with the specifics and we'll discuss whether a separate engagement makes sense at a different price.
```

### "Do you do white-label for agencies?"

```
Yes. Agency white-label runs at the same $2,500 per engagement (you mark up your retail price). Agencies with 5+ concurrent engagements get a sibling tier at a lower per-unit price. Reply for the agency intake form.
```
