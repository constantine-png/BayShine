# Publish notes — Demand probe

## Goal

Validate that there is demand for two products before writing the big
ones (Schema Pack v2 with 24 verticals, GEO Playbook at 60-80 pages).

The probe posts are positioned as "I'm building this, would you buy
it" — transparent, no fake claims, no link-bait.

## Order of operations

1. **IndieHackers post first.** Post to "Ideas and Validation" section.
   IndieHackers community responds within 24-48 hours typically.
2. **r/LocalSEO post 24 hours later.** Reddit doesn't like the same
   content posted across communities simultaneously; staggering looks
   organic.
3. **Monitor for 5 business days.** Track: comments, upvotes, DMs,
   beta-buyer signups (IndieHackers post offers 10 beta slots at $14).
4. **Day 6 decision:**
   - **High interest** (>10 DMs OR >5 beta signups OR >20 upvotes) →
     ship Schema Pack v1 at $29 immediately, start writing GEO
     Playbook with confidence.
   - **Moderate interest** (3-10 DMs OR 2-5 beta signups) → ship
     Schema Pack v1 at $29, defer GEO Playbook write until v1 has
     real sales.
   - **Low interest** (<3 DMs AND <2 beta signups) → re-position.
     The verticals or the price or the framing is wrong. Journal
     entry under operator-info-products lineage identifies which.

## What success looks like

5+ beta-buyer signups inside 5 days means there is a real audience
willing to pay for this. That validates the entire Information
Products archetype direction, not just the Schema Pack.

## What failure looks like

Zero engagement on both posts means either (a) the channel was wrong
or (b) the offer is wrong. The cheap test is to repost to r/SEO and
r/smallbusiness one week later with a slightly different framing
("schema for service businesses" rather than "schema for mobile
service businesses"). If that gets traction, the original framing was
too narrow.

## Voice constraints

The Atrium voice rules apply to all replies in both threads:

- No em-dashes; use a comma or an en-dash with spaces.
- No exclamation marks.
- No "we'd love," "feel free," "passionate about," "exciting."
- No invented numbers.
- Numbers come with sources.

If a commenter asks for sales counts or customer counts before launch,
the honest reply is: "no sales yet, this is the demand probe — that's
why I'm posting before building."

## Voice violation handling

If you (Constantine) catch yourself about to type an exclamation mark
in a reply, replace it with a period. If you catch yourself about to
write "we'd love your feedback," write "feedback welcome" instead.
The brand voice has to start the first time it's heard.
