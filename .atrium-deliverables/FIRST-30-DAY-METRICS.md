These are projections, not promises. The journal's revisited sections at Day 30 mark which projections landed and which missed.

## Day 30 success vs failure summary

### Success scenario (Day 30)

- Atrium revenue: $2,000-$6,000 cumulative.
- Forge revenue: $50-$300 cumulative (mostly newsletter; SaaS revenue starts in Q3 2026 when Citation Watch/Schema Doctor Pro launch).
- Combined newsletter subscribers (Cited Weekly + Forge Weekly): 200-500.
- Combined waitlist signups (Citation Watch + Schema Doctor Pro): 50-150.
- 2-3 customer support questions handled.
- 0-1 refund processed (within policy window).
- At least one published case study (BayShine self-audit or one paying customer).

### Failure scenario (Day 30)

- Atrium revenue: <$500 cumulative.
- Forge revenue: $0 cumulative.
- Newsletter subscribers <50 combined.
- Waitlist signups <15 combined.
- Zero published case studies.
- One or more launch posts that got <5 upvotes/reactions.

If the failure scenario: write a Journal entry on Day 31 under `founding` lineage diagnosing the most likely cause. Three common causes:

1. **Distribution problem.** Posts didn't reach the right audience. Solution: try ProductHunt, Hacker News, different subreddits, X promotion.
2. **Positioning problem.** Audience doesn't see the products as solving their problem. Solution: rewrite sales pages with different framing.
3. **Catalog mismatch.** Audience is the right one but the catalog doesn't match what they want. Solution: re-evaluate the demand probe data; possibly pivot the catalog mix.

Most likely: a mix of all three at lower severity. The journal entry identifies primary cause; subsequent sessions address it.

## Specific Auditor flags by Day 30

The Atrium and Forge Auditors run their first quarterly review at end of Q2 (June 30, 2026), which is roughly Day 50. At Day 30, the Auditors do an interim spot-check on:

### Constitutional adherence

- Has any customer-facing artifact violated voice rules? (em-dash count, exclamation mark count, soft-marketing-phrase count).
- Has any number cited in a published artifact been verifiable from real data?

### Operator performance

- Did the operators (operator-info-products, operator-productized-services, operator-newsletter for Atrium; operator-forge-products for Forge) log their weekly status?
- Did they file monthly summary by Day 30?

### Cross-network coordination

- Did Constantine's bandwidth balance work? Or did one network's emergencies dominate?
- Did shared specialists' dual-voice calibration hold?

### Atrium-style temptation log (Forge-specific)

- Did Forge defer any launch for "more evidence" (drift toward Atrium)?
- Did Forge tone down marketing voice on any artifact (drift toward Atrium)?
- Did Forge avoid named-operator presence on any artifact?

These spot-checks at Day 30 catch drift early so the quarterly review at Day 50 has cleaner data.

## How to use this document

- **Constantine:** Reference at each milestone (Day 7, 14, 21, 30) to check whether progress matches expectation. If significantly under-performing, escalate to the founding agent via "what's failing" question.
- **Both Auditors:** Use the failure thresholds as drift detection signals. Sustained under-performance (3+ consecutive milestones below threshold) triggers a critical-flag in the quarterly briefing.
- **Operators:** Use the metrics as input for monthly summary templates. Operator monthly summary at end of Month 1 should compare actuals against these projections.

The numbers will be wrong on the first run. They're calibration points, not promises. Each subsequent month's projection refines based on the prior month's data.
