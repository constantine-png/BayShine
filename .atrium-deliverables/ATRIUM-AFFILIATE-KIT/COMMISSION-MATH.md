## Why Atrium retains 70% (rather than splitting 50/50)

Affiliate market standard is 20-30% commission for info products. Atrium's 30% is at the high end of this range, deliberately:

- It signals the program is meant to attract serious affiliates (not casual reciprocal-promotion arrangements).
- It compensates affiliates for the brand discipline (no-em-dash-no-soft-marketing constraints) that limits their volume tactics.
- It scales with product price: $750 commission on a $2,500 sale is materially more attractive than $500 commission would be.

A 50/50 split would change Atrium's product margins materially — Schema Pack v1.5 at $19.50 net (after 50% commission) is below the time-cost of supporting the customer. Atrium's discipline holds the 30% rate as the maximum unless an exceptional partner (Gold-tier affiliate with sustained high volume) justifies the 35% bonus.

## Refund handling on affiliate commissions

Gumroad's standard policy: refunded sales reverse the affiliate's commission within the same payout cycle. So if an affiliate-attributed sale refunds within 7 days, the commission is clawed back automatically.

Atrium's policy is the standard 7-day no-questions refund. Affiliates have minimal refund risk because most refunds happen within the policy window.

## Featured-affiliate slot tiering

At Silver tier, affiliates earn one featured-affiliate slot in Cited Weekly per quarter.

What "featured" means:

- One paragraph mention of the affiliate's publication in a relevant Cited Weekly issue.
- Direct link to the affiliate's publication.
- Atrium does NOT pay for this; it's a value-add from the affiliate program.
- Subject to editorial fit (no guarantee of placement in every quarter).

This is the cross-promotion mechanic that compounds the affiliate's audience while keeping editorial integrity intact (the featured affiliate must be a real ongoing partner whose publication is actually useful to Atrium's audience; not a paid sponsorship).

At Gold tier: featured-affiliate slot in Cited Weekly monthly (with editorial-fit caveat) + featured placement on Atrium's content site (when that site exists at Rung 2+) + direct line to Constantine for product feedback.

## Cross-network affiliate dynamics

Forge has its own catalog (Citation Watch, Schema Doctor Pro, future subscriptions, enterprise services). Forge's affiliate strategy is different — subscriptions have different unit economics than info products, and Forge's catalog is targeted at developers and agencies rather than SMB operators.

An affiliate could promote BOTH Atrium products AND Forge products if their audience spans both buyer profiles. But the two networks' affiliate programs are independent. An affiliate signs up separately for each, gets separate commission, separate tracking.

The coordination doc (`Atrium/spine/coordination.md` and Forge mirror) covers this: shared specialists are calendar-coordinated; shared affiliates are independently tracked across both networks.

## What this kit doesn't cover (yet)

- Pre-existing relationships with potential affiliates (Constantine's network beyond what the specialist's research surfaces). Constantine maintains those manually until the kit's process becomes the default.
- Affiliate-driven sponsorship deals (paid sponsorships rather than commission-based). Different model; not in this kit's scope.
- Reseller arrangements (affiliates reselling Atrium products as their own). Not in scope; reseller agreements are case-by-case at Rung 3+.
