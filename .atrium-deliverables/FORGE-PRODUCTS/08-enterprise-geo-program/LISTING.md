## Pricing rationale

$25,000 is Forge's flagship offering. The price reflects:

- 30 business days of intensive work across 20-200 locations.
- 12 months of probe tracking dashboard included (vs Citation Watch at $19/mo × 12 = $228 standalone — but the Program's tracking is multi-location, so the standalone equivalent for 50 locations would be 50 × $228 = $11,400; the Program absorbs that cost).
- 12 monthly strategy calls with Constantine (~ 12 hours of dedicated executive-tier time, valued at $300/hour = $3,600).
- Custom architecture and per-location optimization at scale.
- Executive briefing document for board / leadership reference.

The Program is priced for buyers where AI search-attributable revenue is genuinely meaningful — typically $5M+ annual revenue with significant consumer-facing query volume. At that revenue scale, $25k for AI search infrastructure that drives 5-15% citation lift across all locations is a strong ROI on a 12-month basis.

## Sales motion

Program inquiries are exclusively from:

- Direct email referencing the sales page (mailto link).

- LinkedIn InMail for enterprise targets via Sales Navigator (LINKEDIN-OUTREACH.md variant I1).
- Referrals from completed Sprint engagements (the cross-sell from $5k to $25k for buyers whose footprint grows or who want full multi-location coverage).
- Forge Weekly paid-tier subscriber referrals (Constantine's direct line builds enterprise pipeline).

Program is NOT marketed via:

- Public IndieHackers launch posts (too high-touch for indie audience).
- Reddit (audience mismatch).
- Twitter threads (engagement-level wrong).

## Discovery call structure

45 minutes. Constantine on the call.

- 5 min: introductions, confirm buyer represents decision authority.
- 15 min: scope alignment. Number of locations, primary verticals, current AI search visibility state (we'll have run a sampling probe before the call).
- 10 min: timeline and team. Does 30 days work for buyer's decision velocity? Who internally needs to be involved (CMO, CMO + CIO, board approval)?
- 10 min: scope confirmation and pricing.
- 5 min: next-step alignment. Send engagement charter document.

## Engagement charter

The Program ships with a structured charter document (Word or Notion format; sent post-discovery-call). Sections:

1. Scope (the 8 deliverables from the sales page, explicitly named).
2. Timeline (30-day calendar; phase-by-phase milestones).
3. Success criteria (citation position lift target, defined per engagement based on baseline probe data).
4. Buyer responsibilities (CMS access for all locations, primary contact for Q&A within 12 business hours).
5. Forge responsibilities (delivery on timeline, executive briefing signed by Constantine, monthly call cadence for 12 months).
6. Payment terms (lump sum or 3-payment milestone).
7. Change order process.
8. Refund policy.

Both parties sign before any work begins.

## Operational notes

- Engagement cap per Constantine's calendar: 2 Programs simultaneously (each takes 30 business days with ~70% of his attention; 2 × 70% > 100%, so 2 is the practical upper bound until enterprise-engagement-builder descendant + subcontracted senior team at Rung 5+).
- If 2 active Programs, new inquiries route to 30-60 day scheduling delay.
- Specialists (shared with Atrium) work the Program at `voice: forge` calibration. Higher per-engagement volume of specialist time than Sprint or Setup.

## Cross-network referrals

A Program-qualified buyer who lands at Atrium first (via Atrium content site or AI Citation Audit) should be referred to Forge. The constitutional discipline: a multi-location chain with 20+ locations is a Forge buyer; recommending Atrium's GEO Setup at $2,500 would under-serve them.

The reverse: a buyer who lands at Forge first but is actually single-location and budget-constrained gets honestly recommended to Atrium's Setup. We don't push them to Sprint at $5k when Setup at $2,500 fits better.

This honest cross-network routing is the constitutional discipline both networks apply per the coordination doc.

## 12-month tracking infrastructure

The Program includes the Citation Watch infrastructure at no monthly cost (vs the standard $19/mo subscription). This is the multi-domain version of Citation Watch's monitoring:

- Per-location dashboards.
- Master brand dashboard rolling up location data.
- Weekly probes across Perplexity, ChatGPT search, Claude, Gemini for 25-40 queries per location.
- Real-time alerts on citation drops, schema drift, llms.txt changes.

After the 12-month engagement window, the buyer transitions to:

- Citation Watch standalone at $19/mo per location (multi-location billing arrangement).
- Schema Doctor Pro at $99/mo for ongoing schema management.
- Extended Program retainer at $2,500/quarter (covers continued Constantine strategic calls plus probe tracking infrastructure).

The transition framework is documented in the engagement charter; buyers can decide which post-Program path fits.

## Founding-engagement window

The first 5 Programs lock in $25,000 pricing for life on repeat / expansion engagements. After 5 close, standard pricing review (likely Q1 2027):

- $35,000 if the market supports it.
- $25,000 if competitive pressure or market response indicates we're at ceiling.

## What's not yet built (but referenced)

The 12-month tracking dashboard is real infrastructure that doesn't exist yet — it's Forge Rung 2 build (subscription-product-builder authors it as part of Citation Watch's deployment, then extended for multi-domain in Schema Doctor Pro's build).

For the first 2-3 Programs that close before the dashboard is built, manual tracking via CSV exports and weekly email reports substitutes for the dashboard interface. The engagement charter notes this with a target dashboard delivery date based on Rung 2 trigger timing.
